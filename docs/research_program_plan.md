# Decision-Making Under Systemic Stress
## A Unified Research Program — Markets, Food Systems, and Migration
### (Revised: with the well-posedness verdict integrated)

---

### The one-sentence thesis

Decision-making under systemic stress is structurally different from decision-making under
calm — possibly different enough to require a **non-classical (contextual) probability
theory** to describe. This program *tests* that hypothesis with a single methodological
apparatus across three substrates. A structural result (below) determines *where* the strong
claim can even be posed: the markets develop and stress-test the method and debunk the easy
claim; the cognitive domain is where genuine contextuality can actually exist.

### The unifying scientific question

> Does behavior under **System-1 conditions** (fast, fear-driven, context-dependent) exhibit
> genuine non-classical structure — contextuality that survives correction for
> measurement-dependence — that **System-2 conditions** (slow, deliberative, classical) do not?

### Epistemic stance (non-negotiable)

- We do **not** assume non-classical structure exists. We test for it.
- Quantum probability is a **language and a falsifiable test**, agnostic on physical reality.
- A **null result** is a genuine, publishable finding and a fully acceptable outcome.
- The realistic deliverable is a **map of where** structure appears (regimes, sectors,
  subsets, conditions) — not a blanket "X is quantum."

---

### A structural result that shapes the entire program: where the test is well-posed

Before any data work, ask whether each domain even *admits* a legitimate contextuality test.
A cyclic contextuality system requires not just four contents and four contexts in a cycle,
but the deep property that makes the test non-trivial: **genuine incompatibility** — the
unmeasured content must be a real counterfactual, because measuring one *disturbs* the
others, so the full set cannot be jointly realized.

**Markets fail this — and the failure is by construction, not by data.** In observational
price data, every quantity is jointly observed each day (sign and magnitude of both assets,
simultaneously). There is a single empirical joint distribution; nothing is destroyed by
observing; there are no counterfactuals. The four "regimes" are disjoint **subsamples** of
one joint distribution. Because a single joint distribution exists, the system is
**non-contextual by construction**: any apparent CHSH violation reduces entirely to
*signaling* (subsample-conditional correlations differ via leverage and crisis-coupling),
which the CbD framework correctly assigns to the signaling term Δ. Markets cannot host true
contextuality observationally. (The only escape is *interventional* measurement — trading
that moves the price — a different and much harder experiment.)

**Cognition passes.** For individual judgments, answering one question genuinely disturbs the
state for the next; you cannot obtain the pristine answer to both on one trial; the
"what would they have answered in the other order" is a real, unobserved counterfactual. That
is the measurement-disturbance structure markets lack, and it is the structural prerequisite
for true contextuality.

| Requirement | Markets (observational) | Cognition / evacuation |
|---|---|---|
| Four contents, four contexts, cyclic | ✗ (degenerate 2×4) | ✓ (question orders / framings) |
| Same content across two contexts | same observable, different *subsamples* | same question, genuinely disturbed |
| Incompatibility / counterfactuals | ✗ — all jointly observed | ✓ — answering disturbs |
| Single joint distribution exists? | **Yes, always** → non-contextual | **No** → contextuality possible |
| Can host the *discovery*? | **No** (by construction) | **Yes** (if it exists anywhere) |

This is not a weakness of the program — it is its organizing principle. It assigns each
domain its proper job and preempts the most damaging objection a referee could raise.

### The three tracks (roles assigned by the verdict above)

**Track A — General markets (S&P 500). Method development + deflation + behavioral contrast.
*Not* a contextuality host.**
The apparatus is built and stress-tested here because the data is deep and the crisis/calm
regime structure gives a naturally-occurring System-1/System-2 *behavioral* contrast. Three
jobs: (i) develop and validate the CbD pipeline; (ii) **demonstrate the deflation** — show
rigorously that apparent quantum-finance Bell violations are signaling, a worthwhile,
field-correcting result; (iii) characterize how coupling and marginals differ between
stressed and calm regimes — real econometrics, the aggregate footprint whose *mechanism*
Track C tests. The expected and principled outcome of the contextuality test here is
"everything deflates to signaling."

**Track B — Food-system markets and trade networks. The domain hinge.**
The matured method applied where food-security stakes are high and the cleanest exogenous
instruments exist (weather, conflict surprises). Same epistemic status as Track A
(method + deflation + behavioral characterization), now in the food domain and connected
directly to the existing 2022 wheat-price-crisis modeling. Its contextuality status is the
same as Track A's — non-contextual by construction — so its contribution is domain insight
and the instrument/food-security story, not a contextuality claim.

**Track C — Migration / evacuation decisions. The cognitive core and the *sole* home of the
contextuality discovery.**
The only domain with genuine incompatibility, hence the only place the strong claim can be
posed. Individual decisions under acute stress, tested with incompatible-question / order-
effect designs under System-1 vs System-2 induction, and modeled with FLEE using System-1
(quantum-like) decision rules. This is where "is stressed cognition non-classical?" becomes a
real, answerable question.

---

### Why food systems is the hinge, not a third silo

Food-system shocks are **simultaneously** market phenomena (commodity and agricultural-equity
prices — the Track-A method applies) and migration drivers (food insecurity → displacement —
the Track-C cognitive core). Food systems therefore sits at the **structural intersection** of
the other two tracks. The same systemic-stress event (e.g., a Black Sea disruption) is a price
shock *and* a displacement driver *and* a decision-under-fear event — one phenomenon at three
scales. This is the keystone that makes the portfolio one program rather than three.

### The two tests, correctly located

**On markets (Tracks A/B): the crisis-vs-calm test as DEFLATION + behavioral contrast.**
Construct the CbD system; estimate it separately in crisis and calm windows; compute
`s_odd = max(|S₁|,…,|S₄|)`, the signaling term Δ, and `s_odd − Δ` in each regime. Given joint
observability, the principled expectation is that **both regimes deflate** (`s_odd − Δ ≤ 2`):
no contextuality, the crisis/calm difference fully explained by signaling. The value is not a
contextuality finding but (i) a validated method, (ii) a quantitative debunking of apparent
quantum-finance violations, and (iii) a real behavioral characterization of how stressed-
regime coupling differs from calm — the aggregate System-1/System-2 *behavioral* contrast,
stated as classical.

**In cognition (Track C): the System-1/System-2 asymmetric CONTEXTUALITY test.**
Here the strong claim is legitimate. Administer an incompatible-question design (e.g., binary
judgments: threat severe? evacuation feasible? trust the order? are others leaving? — in
varied orders/framings) under two induced conditions: System-1 (time pressure, arousal, load)
and System-2 (deliberation, time, decision aids). Run CbD in each. The fast/slow hypothesis
predicts an **asymmetry**:

1. **Both non-contextual** → null; order effects are signaling (consistent with prior
   literature). A clean, reportable result.
2. **System-1 contextual, System-2 not** → the discovery: stressed cognition requires a
   different probability theory than deliberative cognition. The headline claim of the whole
   program, and the only place it can legitimately arise.
3. **Both contextual** → surprising; scrutinize design and stationarity before any claim.

**Robustness, both tests:** synthetic-first (a flexible classical/regime-switching model must
*fail* to reproduce any claimed contextuality, or it is an artifact); always report how much
of the raw `s_odd` survives the Δ subtraction; stratify (markets: by sector/subset; cognition:
by subject and arousal level); placebo (randomized regime/condition labels must show nothing).

### Development trajectory

**Phase 1 — Method + deflation (Track A).** Resolve the cyclic-system construction; build the
CbD pipeline on the existing four-S code; run the crisis-vs-calm test on the S&P 500; stratify
by sector. Deliverable: a methods-and-deflation paper establishing the apparatus and showing
how much apparent quantum-finance signal is signaling (with "deflates to signaling" as the
expected result).

**Phase 2 — Domain (Track B).** Apply the method to agricultural-equity and wheat-trade data,
exploiting the weather/conflict instruments, connected to the existing wheat-crisis work.
Deliverable: a food-system paper that is both a market-behavior characterization and a
food-security contribution.

**Phase 3 — Mechanism + the discovery (Track C).** Bring the validated apparatus and the
System-1/System-2 framing to individual migration/evacuation decisions, via behavioral data
where obtainable and via FLEE with quantum-like System-1 rules. Deliverable: the cognitive-
core study — the one place the asymmetric contextuality hypothesis is a legitimate, direct
claim about human judgment under duress.

Logic: **method → domain → mechanism**, data-rich/aggregate to data-poor/individual, with food
systems the hinge keeping all three coupled. Note the inversion worth being explicit about:
the data is richest where the strong claim is impossible (markets), and the strong claim is
possible only where data is scarce (cognition) — which is *why* the markets phase exists, to
build and validate the instrument before the data-poor decisive test.

### How this reads at tenure review

One **program**: a single question (does stressed cognition require a different probability
theory?), a single method (contextuality testing with a fast/slow interpretation), three
substrates with assigned roles and a clear trajectory, and an explicit epistemic
stance. The well-posedness verdict is a feature in the dossier: **stating up front that
markets are non-contextual by construction — that apparent financial Bell violations are
signaling — preempts the single most damaging referee objection and demonstrates rigor**, while
locating the genuine discovery precisely where it can live. The portfolio absorbs the existing
record (the wheat-crisis work becomes Phase-2 groundwork; the agent-based and quantum-game-
theory work becomes the Track-C modeling foundation). The food-system hinge is the sentence
that makes a committee see coherence: *food, finance, and migration are one phenomenon —
decision-making under systemic stress — observed at three scales.*

### Risks (named before a reviewer names them)

- **The discovery may not be there.** Even in cognition, prior CbD work mostly found
  non-contextuality (signaling). The fast/slow asymmetry is a genuine, falsifiable bet with a
  skeptical prior; Phase 3 may return a null. That is an acceptable, publishable outcome and
  the program does not depend on a positive result.
- **Markets are method, not discovery — by construction, now resolved.** This is no longer an
  open risk but a settled structural fact, incorporated above. The risk would be *forgetting*
  it and over-claiming contextuality in markets; the plan guards against that explicitly.
- **Aggregation inference.** Reading aggregate market behavior as a footprint of individual
  System-1 cognition is a *hypothesis* Track C is designed to test, not an established fact.
  State it as such.
- **Migration data scarcity.** Mitigated by building the method in the data-rich markets phase
  and by using FLEE as a controlled substrate; behavioral-experiment data for the decisive test
  is the genuine bottleneck and should be planned for early.
